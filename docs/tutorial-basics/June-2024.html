"If humans crawled on their elbows for generations, would natural selection give humans elbow caps? Just like knee caps?"

Bro, it's luck. If the only way to survive for humans, is by having elbow caps, then yes. Natural selection is natural because only the ones who survive pass on their genes. So if chance has it, and over time the ones with elbow protection survive more often than those without protection, then maybe after generations we could have elbow caps. That is if luck has it "we" evolve and have an elbow cap. So no, natural selection would not give us elbow caps if enough generations crawled on their elbows. But it could be possible in certain environments. I suggest learning on how evolution happens, it will open up your mind.

So how could we gain elbow caps? Supposedly by gene editing, or by forcing mutation with gene altering thing, or by subjecting humans to experiments in different environments to see which ones over time evolve into "elbow cap having humans". When a group of a species is isolated, it "adapts" to its environment through the death and birth of many of it's kind. Till one of it's kind devolops (has) a trait that let's it have a higher chance of survival. If we were to set the experiments setting to be one where the test subject species would need elbow caps to survive, we could force an evolution through natural processes.

Though, I mean, back to your original question. If the only thing humans knew how to do (for mobility), was to crawl on their elbows, then yes. As I said, if the only way of movement we had was to crawl on our elbows, we would be dead if we couldn't move (crawl on our elbows). This situation could be a death sentence in a hostile environment, so to combat this, you must be able to crawl on your elbows (move). If you can't, you die, and if you can you survive you pass on genes. The ones who survive reproduce and pass on their genes, which would make people with the ability to elbow crawl more common, as the ones who couldn't elbow crawled died out, leaving less of those type of people. And if we take this a step further, by making the environment have a harsh terrain, we could "force" them to evolve in a way that solves the need for protection of the elbow while crawling on the rough ground. So, they would die if their elbows broke while crawling, as they would get caught by predators. So to combat this, they must pray that someone is born with an elbow protecting trait such as the elbow cap. If such an event happens, the one with the elbow cap trait would have a higher chance of survival, meaning it would reproduce more often than the ones without the trait. Leading to the population of elbow cap having humans increase.

So in a way, yes, it is possible. I do think that nature is cruel though, only the ones who survive pass on their genes. To survive you must be lucky enough to be born with an advantageous trait, or you are destined to perish.
