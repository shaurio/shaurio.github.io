Just read about linear and exponential relationship. Linear is where the increase is proportial or the same as last time, it is linear. Exponential is where the value exponetially increases, such as:

Jake wants to double his pencil amount every month. But he does not know how to represent it, he goes to ask a friend.

Answer the following:

You are the friend, find a way to represent the amount if Jake starts with 10 pencils

The answer should be an equation/function (1 * (2/3 ^t) or f(x) = x * (2/3 ^t )

So, what does this even mean? Jake wants to double his pencil amount every month, okay. So this guy wants to double (x2) the amount of pencils he has every month. But he does not know how to represent it, so he goes to ask a friend (us). Alright, this is how I would represent it:

  • 10 * 2^t

Why? Because he starts with 10 pencils, and he wants to double them, so " * 2 ". And he wants to do it each month, so the amount of pencils would exponentially increase every month. So it would look like if " t "/# of months is three:

  • f(3) = 10 * 2^t
  • f(3) = 10 * 2^3
  • f(3) = 10 * (222)
  • f(3) = 10 * 8
  • f(3) = 80

In three months he would have 80 pencils, is this correct? Yep, pretty much. So 10 becomes 20 on month one because 10 * 2 is = 20. And then at two months it would be 20 doubled which would be 40. At three months, 80. At four months 160, and so on. Get it? I did not mean to make it into a function, it was just easier to represent if I did. This solved the question, nice. Anyways, I will continue to study. I will make a graph to show how it increases.
